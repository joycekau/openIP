# oneIP — Brand & Product Playbook

**Tokenize your influence.**

_A go-to-market handbook for Product & Marketing Managers._

</div>

> **Who this is for.** Product Managers and Marketing Managers promoting the oneIP brand to the right audiences to grow **brand awareness, followers, and revenue**.
> **Scope note.** Customer-facing prices (what a fan or creator actually pays — see §8a) are included so you can quote them in campaigns. What's intentionally **excluded** is the internal economics: how we split revenue, calculate margins, cost/retail markups, and partner commissions — those stay with the management team.

## 1. The one-liner

**oneIP turns a creator's audience into an on-chain asset — and makes crypto invisible to the fans who fund it.**

Fans shop, tip, and unlock content in ordinary money on a beautiful, Pinterest-style storefront. Behind the scenes, a share of every sale flows into the creator's on-chain value pool with a floor that _can't go to zero_. Traders and superfans get a gmgn-style terminal to discover and back creators early.

One brand, **two front doors**, one identity:

| | **oneIP.ai** | **oneIP.io** |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience** | Everyday fans & buyers | Creators, superfans & traders |
| **Feels like** | A creator marketplace + social feed | A crypto trading & launch terminal |
| **Crypto visible?** | **No** — pure fiat, zero jargon | **Yes** — wallets, charts, on-chain |
| **Job it does** | Convert attention → revenue | Convert belief → early backing |

## 2. Why now — the problem we solve

Creators today are stuck between two broken options:

- **Ad/platform economy** — they build the audience, the platform keeps the upside, and the algorithm can switch off their reach overnight.
- **Crypto/creator coins** — high upside, but scammy, volatile, "goes to zero," and impossible for a normal fan to touch.

**oneIP's wedge:** give creators a *real business* (commerce + content + community) that a normal fan can use with zero crypto knowledge — while quietly compounding on-chain value that early believers can back. The fan never has to know there's a token. The creator never has to choose between "safe income" and "upside."

## 3. Product propositions (value props by audience)

Use these as the backbone of landing pages, decks, and ad copy. Each is a *promise + proof*.

### For creators / KOLs — _"Own your audience. Never start from zero."_
- **One-click everything** — launch a store, post content, and mirror it to up to 14 platforms from a single studio.
- **Get paid three ways** — sell products (physical/digital), unlock premium content, and receive tips & paid reactions.
- **A value pool that grows with your business** — a slice of your revenue continuously reinforces your creator pool's floor, on-chain and publicly verifiable.
- **Credibility that travels** — your logo and socials render natively across major wallets and explorers, so you look legitimate everywhere.
- **Free to start** — become a creator, post, and open a value pool with no wallet and no upfront cost. Going fully on-chain is opt-in.

### For fans / buyers — _"Support creators you love — no crypto required."_
- **Shop a gorgeous feed** — a Pinterest-style waterfall of products and creator work, buy or unlock in one tap.
- **Pay in normal money** — familiar fiat checkout, localized for Southeast Asia.
- **Express yourself** — follow, comment, subscribe, tip, and send paid reactions.
- **Discover the "buy-the-look" moment** — creator work links straight to the products behind it.

### For superfans / traders — _"Back the creators everyone else is about to notice."_
- **gmgn-style terminal** — trending creators + whole-market Solana meme discovery in one screen.
- **Smart-money & KOL tracking** — see what proven wallets are doing, with real PnL.
- **A floor you can verify** — the "never-zero" mechanic is enforced on-chain, not promised in a pitch deck.
- **Safety signals built in** — security badges and verification surfaced before you act.

### For brands / suppliers / factories — _"Put your products in front of creators who sell."_
- **Supply-side onboarding** — brands, factories, and supply chains list offers and products.
- **Creators do the selling** — link a brand and its products drop straight into creator stores.
- **Performance-native** — you reach engaged audiences through trusted voices, not cold ads.

### For regional channel partners — _"Grow the network in your market, earn for real results."_
- **Authorized regional partners** help onboard creators and brands in their country/region.
- Rewards are **channel-service commissions and refer-a-friend incentives** tied to *genuine* transactions — a partner program, not a recruiting scheme.

## 8. Growth playbook (followers & revenue)

**North-star framing:** every creator we activate brings their own audience. Growth compounds when creators become distribution.

- **Creator-led acquisition (primary loop):** recruit creators → they bring fans → fans become buyers → some become creators. Invest marketing where it *activates creators*.
- **Two funnels, matched to intent:**
  - _Fan funnel (oneIP.ai):_ social-first, product/work showcases, "buy-the-look," creator spotlights. Optimize for first purchase + follow.
  - _Trader funnel (oneIP.io):_ crypto-native channels, terminal/leaderboard content, "verifiable floor" explainers, early-backer narratives.
- **Content engine:** creator success stories, before/after "I started at zero," behind-the-scenes launches, KOL leaderboards, "how the floor works" explainers (utility framing only — see §9).
- **Partnerships:** regional creator networks/MCNs, brand & supply-chain partners, and authorized regional partners as an on-the-ground growth arm.
- **Localization as growth:** ship campaigns natively in the 7 supported languages; don't translate — localize the hook per market.
- **Referral & community:** refer-a-friend and community rewards tied to real activity (framed as partner/loyalty programs, never recruiting).

**Metrics PMs & marketers should watch** (directional — not the internal economics):
activated creators · creator retention · fans per creator · first-purchase rate · repeat-purchase rate · content posted → distributed · value-pool activations · terminal engagement · follower growth per campaign.

## 8a. Customer-facing pricing (safe to quote)

These are prices a fan or creator actually sees and pays — fine to use in campaigns and product copy. **Do not** pair them with any explanation of how revenue is split, how margins are set, or what partners earn.

| What the customer pays | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tips & paid reactions** | 👍 $0.5 · ✌️ $1 · 💪 $2 · ❤️ $5 · 🔥 $10 | Fans send these on posts, products, and creator work |
| **Become a creator / open a value pool / post** | **Free** | No wallet, no upfront cost — this is a headline hook |
| **Buy products / unlock content** | Set by the creator | Familiar fiat checkout, localized for SEA |
| **Go fully on-chain (mint your token)** | ~0.1 SOL one-time (+ small network rent) | Optional; only when a creator activates on-chain |
| **Trading a creator coin** | 1.25% per trade | Standard swap fee shown at trade time |

**Public product mechanics you can also cite** (these are features, not internal economics):
- On a buy, a portion routes into the creator's floor treasury; on a sell, a small tax also flows *into* the floor.
- Holders can redeem their share of the treasury at any time — the source of the "never-zero" promise.

> Everything about *how those flows are divided* (revenue splits, margins, partner commissions) stays internal — don't publish it.

## 9. ⚠️ Compliance guardrails — read before writing any copy

These are **non-negotiable** for all outbound messaging. They keep the brand on the right side of the line.

**On value & the floor — always frame as _utility_:**
- ✅ Say: "revenue → protocol buyback → **raises the utility floor** (a protocol mechanism)."
- ❌ Never say or imply: dividends, profit-sharing, "holders get paid," IPO/listing, guaranteed returns, or investment gains. **Never make it sound like a security.**

**On the partner program — always frame as _channel/loyalty_:**
- ✅ Say: "authorized regional partner," "channel-service commission," "refer-a-friend reward."
- ❌ Never say: "downline," "recruit members," "levels/tiers of recruits," or anything resembling MLM.

**On the fan experience:**
- ✅ Lead with commerce, community, and creators.
- ❌ Don't open a fan-facing message with tokens, coins, or trading.

When in doubt, describe the *product benefit and mechanism*, not a financial outcome. Route any edge-case claim to management before publishing.

- **Personality:** confident, warm, creator-first. Empowering, not hype-y. Premium but accessible — a "noble" dark-and-gradient aesthetic.
- **Voice by door:** oneIP.ai speaks *creator & lifestyle*; oneIP.io speaks *sharp, on-chain, data-savvy*. Same brand, different register.

- **Do:** be transparent, celebrate creators, show real mechanics.
